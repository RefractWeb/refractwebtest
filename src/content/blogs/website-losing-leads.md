## The Numbers Are Worse Than You Think

The average website converts somewhere between 2% and 5% of its visitors. The top 10% of sites convert at 11.5% or higher.

That gap is not explained by better products, better prices, or better traffic. It is explained by better design decisions.

## The 5 Conversion Killers

- No clear next step above the fold.
- Social proof is hidden or missing entirely.
- Slow load times are bleeding conversions.
- The value proposition is unclear.
- The form is asking too much.

## What to Actually Do About It

You do not need a full redesign to fix most of this. Start with an honest audit of clarity, CTA visibility, proof, mobile speed, and form friction.

The difference between a 2% site and a 10% site is the clarity, speed, and trust of the experience in front of it.
